About

I am a computer scientist, researcher, and student at Northwestern University in Evanston, IL. I live in Austin, TX and will be working on full stack and end-to-end platform features at RigUp Inc., a company dedicated to improving efficiency of the energy industry through digitizing operations. Previously, I was a part of the Array of Things research project group at Argonne National Laboratory, where my research on time-sensitive database solutions and machine learning sensor calibration allowed for more efficient organization and analysis of environmental sensor data.

Recent Projects

Multicraft

I have been working on this project with guidance from Professor Marcelo Worsley at tiilt. The goal of the project is to add more modes of interaction and potentially replace the mouse and keyboard with Gaze and Speech to empower individuals with physical impairments to play and create in Minecraft. My implementation included adding Gaze interaction for the project, for which you can see the current repository here.

Time Series Analysis of Environmental Data

I worked on this project throughout my internship at Argonne, which involved designing a new PostgreSQL database extended with TimescaleDB to handle all of the time-series data gathered by nodes maintained and used by the Array of Things research group. This was then used in my work to create a sensor calibration model from a combination of supervised and unsupervised machine learning models using Python and scikit-learn. The new model lowered reported temperature error by about 60%.

DuraCot

Working in a small team of students, we designed and developed a collapsible changing table to facilitate diaper changing of a 9-year old with cerebral palsy. The portable changing table allows the caretaker to change the diaper within the privacy of a handicap stall of most public restrooms.
